Why name use by federal agencies matters...

Recreation in Washington State is at an all-time high. Federal land management agencies, such as the U.S. Forest Service and the National Park Service manage the lands encompassing the Cascade volcanos and their environmental interpretive materials and programs greatly influence the public. As more hikers access this land and visit the ranger stations of these agencies, they have a greater responsibility to educate the public on its history and future. How do their materials (maps, ranger station exhibits, online resources) and programs tell the stories of Washington's Indigenous tribes, both past and present?
